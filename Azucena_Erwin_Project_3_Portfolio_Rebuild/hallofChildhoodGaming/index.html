Video Games from my Childhood

Here are the Video games from my Childhood that I played when I was young.

Crash Twinsanity

This game is one of the few video games that I play during my childhood in the 2000s.
Its hold a special place in my heart because it broke the mold of previous games with its bizarre humor filled with slapstick and witty dialogue that made me laugh revisiting it,
experimental open-world levels where I can play around, and a unique acapella soundtrack that's actually catchy.
and interestingly, what makes it so memorable to me is the flaws itself due it being a rushed project for its September release date in 2004.

Tekken 5

Tekken is one of the two fighting games I played in my childhood alongside Mortal Kombat Armageddon.
I consider it the fighting game of the cornerstone of my childhood because of its because of its, rapid and fluid gameplay, extensive unlockable content, and memorable soundtrack.
And as a high-octane 3D fighter, it delivered a polished experience that was accessible for casual play, yet deep enough to create lasting rivalries.
Not to mention its jam packed with high-quality pre-rendered FMV endings for every character, custom character customization, and even playable arcade versions of Tekken 1, 2, and 3..

Wario Land 4

Wario Land 4 is a favorite of the handheld games I played for its unique "weirdness," unsettling atmosphere, and high-quality production.
It focuses on exploration and momentum, allowing me to enjoy the game through multiple playthroughs to collect all treasures, rather than just clearing the level once
For me it's one of the first platformers I play alongside Crash Twinsanity where unlike Twinsanity is a 2d platformer that focus on transforming, accumulating treasure, and racing against a timer to exit the level after activating the "frog switch," creating a unique sense of urgency.
and that's not mentioning that it felt like a complete product meaning that I get to play through the whole experiences.
